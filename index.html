Quito, Ecuador

Quito holds the title of the second-highest capital city in the world, nestled in the Andes Mountains at an elevation of 2,850 meters (9,350 feet) above sea level. This gives it a unique climate and stunning views.
Quito's historic center is one of the largest and best-preserved in Latin America. It was the first city to be declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1978, recognized for its well-preserved colonial architecture and rich history.
